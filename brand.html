01 — Identity

The visual
grammar of precision.

The NeolabCare identity is built on the contrast between matte textures and hairline gold. A system designed to feel like a high-end laboratory instrument — not a skincare brand.

"Quiet. Precise. Enduring."
— Brand Positioning Statement
Matte Black Foundation
The brand lives on #0A0A0A — not a generic dark, but a precise, warm matte black. Every surface is a variation of this base. No white backgrounds. No light modes.
Sand Gold as Structure
Gold (#C8B89A) is used exclusively as a structural line — borders, hairlines, accent rules, and eyebrow labels. It is never used as a fill, a background, or a CTA colour.
Serif Italic for Emphasis
Noto Serif Display italic is used only within headlines — never as body copy. It signals the emotional register of a word or phrase. It is used once per headline, never twice.
No Colour on Screen
The red accent (crimson silk lining, bottle dot, inner label) exists only in the physical world. On screen, the brand is black, white, and gold line. No red. No blue. No green. The physical object is the reveal.
Bottega Veneta Register
The reference is Bottega Veneta: no colour, no noise, just the brand. Confidence through restraint. The identity communicates authority by what it withholds, not by what it displays.

04 — Voice & Tone

Precise.
Never performative.

The NeolabCare voice is the voice of someone who has done the work and does not need to prove it. Confident without being aggressive. Intelligent without being academic. Warm without being casual.

Write this
✓Short sentences. One idea per sentence.
✓Active voice. "We made a different decision." Not "A different decision was made."
✓Specific over vague. "Produced fresh to order" not "freshly made."
✓Acknowledge uncertainty when it exists. "Projected" not "proven."
✓Let the product speak. Describe what it does, not how special it is.
Not this
✗"World's first," "revolutionary," "game-changing" — overclaims that undermine trust.
✗Exclamation marks. The brand does not shout.
✗Superlatives without evidence. "The most potent" requires a citation.
✗Wellness language. "Journey," "ritual," "transform your skin."
✗Hedging. "May help support" — if the evidence is weak, don't make the claim.

07 — Formula Reference

Every active.
Carefully selected.

The formula is the brand. Every key active was selected against published research. This section is the reference layer — for communications, claims review, and partner briefings.

β-Nicotinamide Mononucleotide (Liposome-Encapsulated)
Supports NAD+ pathways associated with cellular energy and sirtuin activity. Liposome encapsulation selected for dermal penetration at therapeutic concentration.
Rajman et al., Cell Metabolism, 2018 · Mills et al., Cell Metabolism, 2016
Acetyl Hexapeptide-8 (Argireline)
Neuromodulator that inhibits SNARE complex formation. 28-day trials show up to 30% reduction in dynamic expression line depth.
Blanes-Mira et al., Int. J. Cosmetic Science, 2002
Copper Tripeptide-1 (GHK-Cu)
Supports collagen and elastin synthesis. Selected for post-shave micro-wound recovery and anti-inflammatory properties.
Pickart et al., J. Biomaterials Science, 2015
Ergothioneine
Cellular antioxidant with a dedicated transporter (OCTN1) in human skin. Selected for mitochondrial protection against oxidative stress.
Cheah et al., Redox Biology, 2016
Glycyrrhizin (Glabridin)
Inhibits tyrosinase activity and the IL-17 inflammatory axis. Suppresses NF-κB pathway to reduce post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation and redness.
Yokota et al., Pigment Cell Research, 1998
Tranexamic Acid
Blocks the inflammation-to-pigmentation pathway at the plasminogen receptor level. Clinically validated for post-acne and UV-induced hyperpigmentation.
Ebrahimi & Naeini, J. Research Medical Sciences, 2014
Panthenol (Pro-Vitamin B5)
Accelerates epidermal regeneration and wound closure. Reduces transepidermal water loss (TEWL). Selected for post-shave soothing and barrier repair.
Ebner et al., Skin Pharmacology & Physiology, 2002
Natural Squalane + Jojoba Oil
Biomimetic lipid system that supplements the skin's natural sebum without clogging pores. Jojoba bidirectionally regulates sebaceous secretion.
Huang et al., Int. J. Molecular Sciences, 2018
Freshness & Stability Note
NMN: Half-life ~7 months at 20°C (first-order kinetics). Betsuno et al., J Cosmet Dermatol, 2025. PMC12048825.
GHK-Cu: ~45% loss by month 6 in aqueous emulsion. Badenhorst et al., Int J Pharm Sci, 2016. PubMed 25384620.
Vitamin C: ~30% loss in month 1, ~65% by month 6 in aqueous emulsion. Yin et al., Molecules, 2022. PMC8773188.
Hyaluronic Acid MW: 71–95% degradation over extended aqueous storage. Simulescu et al., Carbohydrate Polymers, 2016.
08 — Design Principles

The rules that
cannot be broken.

These are not guidelines. They are constraints. Every design decision is tested against them.

PRINCIPLE 01
Restraint is the message.
Every element that is not present communicates as much as every element that is. If something can be removed without losing meaning, remove it.
PRINCIPLE 02
Gold is structural, not decorative.
The sand gold accent exists only as a hairline — borders, rules, eyebrow labels. It is never used as a fill, a gradient, or a background. The moment it becomes decorative, it loses authority.
PRINCIPLE 03
One serif italic per headline.
Noto Serif Display italic is used once per heading — never twice. It marks the emotional register of a single word or phrase. Overuse destroys its effect.
PRINCIPLE 04
No colour on screen.
The red lives in the physical world only. On screen, the brand is black, white, and gold line. Introducing colour on screen dilutes the impact of the physical reveal.
PRINCIPLE 05
Claims require citations.
Every efficacy claim must be traceable to a published study. If the evidence is weak, the claim is not made. Credibility is built through precision, not assertion.
PRINCIPLE 06
Maximum dedication. Ultimate simplicity.
The founding philosophy is also the design philosophy. Every component, every page, every interaction should do more while asking less. Complexity is a failure of design.
