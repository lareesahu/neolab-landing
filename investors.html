02

The Problem

The routine is broken.
So is the supply chain.

The average premium skincare consumer buys 5–7 separate products to complete a routine. By the time they reach the bathroom shelf, active ingredients have already degraded for months — sometimes years — in warehouses, retailers, and in-transit inventory.

Routine Complexity

Consumers manage 5–7 products across serums, toners, moisturizers, eye creams, and SPF. 68% abandon their routine within 3 months.

Ingredient Degradation

NMN, peptides, and vitamin derivatives lose up to 40% potency on shelves. Consumers pay premium prices for products that have already expired biologically.

No Subscription Intelligence

Existing subscription models ship the same mass-manufactured SKU every month. No freshness guarantee. No batch-aware replenishment. No moat.

03

The Solution & Business Logic

One SKU.
Total routine.
Subscription-native.

A single, 9-function formula produced to confirmed orders and dispatched within 7 days. No retail inventory means zero working-capital tied up in shelf stock. No stabilizers means potency degrades on a 90-day clock — which is exactly the subscription trigger interval. The product architecture and the business model are the same decision.

Why this matters commercially: Most beauty subscriptions are a billing layer on top of a commodity SKU — customers can cancel without loss. Here, cancelling means running out of a product they can't stockpile. Retention is structural, not promotional.
